# Memory Aid Project (Memoria)

## Overall Description

A piece of software that will help people with memory problems (Alzheimer's, dementia, etc.) stay in contact with reality. They will have an **app**, **iPad**, and **interactive mirror** that will remind them every morning when they wake up who they are, who their family is, what they did the past week, what they're going to do, etc.

It will be a **real-time context generator** that fills in the gaps of memory: combining modern devices (tablets, interactive mirrors, smart glasses) with the user's digital database (photographs, contacts, past and future events).

---

## General Things to Consider

- **🔴 MOST IMPORTANT — Keep It Simple:**
  Since we're dealing with people who struggle with memory, the biggest rule is to **KEEP THE SOFTWARE AND UI AS SIMPLE AS POSSIBLE** (for the layout, interface, wording, sound, etc.)

- **🎯 At the End of the Day:**
  The goal is not to cure memory diseases, simply to **aid**. Do as much as you can to help, not to try and fix. This could change depending on medical research throughout the process, or technological research throughout the process (AI might take over the world).

- **📊 It Is a Spectrum:**
  There are degrees of how bad it is — try to incorporate a **level system of mental ability** to cater to someone's degree of memory dysfunction.

- **🤝 Keep in Mind — Co-Users Are Essential:**
  All users **NEED** a co-user to at least help them input data. The worse the patient's brain/memory is, the more aid that may be needed by their in-person helper.

- **🧠 This Combines Human Emotional Intelligence with AI:**
  A co-user is needed to input the data because they understand the person, their relationships, how they interact with the world, and who they are. AI (right now at least) can only use hard data to find patterns and figure out things algorithmically. Yes, many people are diagnosed when it is not that bad so it will be easier for them to do it themselves (mom's point), but things will have to be updated as they get older and progressively have worse memory, so a co-user is very important.

- **🗣️ Tell Me About Your Day:**
  This function will have the user throughout their day talk/type what they did, how they felt about it, and just talk about the things they do and enjoy. At the end of the day, the user will try their best to recall how the day went (this is a long shot for some), but regardless — since they talked throughout the day, their day will be recounted to them through the software.

- **🔊 It Is Easier to Listen Than to Read:**
  Try to keep it **audio intensive**. It will be easier and simpler/more seamless if it is something that can be used passively. Having to be glued to a screen at the end of the day would be kind of going backwards — we don't want to brainwash, we want to help keep their brain functioning well.

- **🧩 Brain Stimulation Keeps the Brain Alive:**
  To my knowledge at the moment, a brain that is more stimulated/educated ends up having a better life and lasting longer. Include **brain stimulation activities** (games, news, articles, stories, books, etc.) to keep the brain going every day.

- **🍳 Cooking Function:**
  Abuela struggles to cook because she forgets if she did certain things already or not. This cooking function will be activated at the user's command and the user will tell it the things they are doing as they cook. It will provide a **visual layout** (words on a screen) **OR audio** (repeats back what has been done) showing the user what they have done while they are cooking. If ovens, air fryers, toasters, etc. are used, the software will tell them to turn them off. This function can be translated and manipulated to be used for many different activities — try to brainstorm.

- **❤️ People Are Lonely:**
  1. Figure out a way for lonely people to be able to use this — some people don't have anyone to help them set it up or work it with them.
  2. Users can maybe connect with each other — support groups, new friends, other users that are in the same area, users of the same memory difficulty level. Have users try to form a **community**. Social interaction is incredibly important for the brain.

- **🎨 Color Scheme:**
  Cool purple.

## How to Get Started

1. Input data for the user.
2. Have user data be nicely formatted and spit back to the user.
3. Translate this to an app-like setting where, for example, pushing a button provides them with a set of daily data about their life/persona (what they would first be reminded of in the morning).
4. Try to get this down perfectly and try to make it as simple as possible over and over for perfection.
5. Include audio — have the program/software/app speak. It is always easier to listen than to read.
